# SafePlay Pro — Project Briefing

## Client Overview
- **Company:** SafePlay Pro
- **Website:** safeplaypro.com
- **Location:** Utah (serves Wasatch Front and broader Intermountain West)
- **Agency role:** SEO strategy, competitive analysis, messaging and positioning, lead generation, web

## What They Do
Utah-based sports surface contractor. Primary services:
- Pickleball court surfacing and construction
- Tennis court surfacing and resurfacing
- Running track construction and maintenance
- Synthetic turf installation
- G-max testing (impact attenuation testing for surface safety compliance)

## Key Differentiators (currently underplayed on site — lead with these)
- **Utah Jazz project** — selected to renovate the original Utah Jazz community court. Elite commercial credibility no competitor can match.
- **G-max testing** — very few Utah competitors offer this. It's a compliance/safety service that opens institutional doors.
- **Running tracks** — a niche most Utah competitors ignore entirely. Underserved keyword territory.
- **UFOMA/UIAAA relationships** — B2B conference angle no competitor is currently working.

## Target Audiences
Split between two distinct segments — messaging should address both:

**B2B (institutional):**
- Facility managers
- School athletic directors
- Municipalities and parks departments
- HOAs with shared courts
- Commercial property owners

**Residential:**
- Homeowners adding backyard pickleball or tennis courts

## Competitors (Utah market)
1. courtsunlimitedut.com — 25+ years, broad Utah market, post-tension concrete specialty
2. parkintennis.com — since 1978, Wasatch Front and Intermountain West
3. precisepickleballcourts.com — family-owned, SportMaster certified, pickleball-first
4. utahcourtsurfacing.com — newer entrant, pickleball-first positioning
5. acecourtsutah.com — 25 years, differentiates on "no outside contractors"

## Site Issues to Address
- Blog last updated 2017 — major SEO and trust problem
- G-max testing, running tracks, and Utah Jazz project are all buried — need to lead with these
- B2B vs residential audiences not clearly separated in messaging or navigation
- Weak CTA structure — no clear lead capture or quote request flow

## Brand Notes
- Tone: professional and credentialed for B2B; approachable and confident for residential
- Lead with commercial credibility (Jazz project) even in residential contexts — it builds trust
- Avoid generic contractor language — every competitor says "quality" and "experience"
- Specific proof beats vague claims: "G-max certified" beats "safety-focused"

## Key CTAs to Use
- "Get a Free Estimate"
- "Request G-max Testing"
- "Start Your Court Project"

## Do Not
- Reference blog content dated 2017 as current
- Lead with "family-owned" or "years of experience" without specific proof
- Use pickleball-only framing — running tracks and G-max testing are differentiators worth featuring prominently
